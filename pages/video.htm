Installation Art

Projection Mapping

One of the more contemporary methods of Peter's art practice is his projection art. Using a video projector and a wraping the video with a 3D mesh in Adobe After Effects, Peter can completely alter any environment. Combining sculpture, projection, and painting, his early 2017 project simply titled "Space Projection"  was unlike anything else he had ever made.

Not only did he scult the helmet and create the mapped video animation, he also added a small speaker inside of the helmet itself. It has a breathing sound on loop that gives the entire project a sense of depth and emmersion that can not be achieved in a single painting.

Large Sculpture

Installation art became a very interesting subject for Peter in 2016. His fascination with changing the space his work was shown could best be accomplished when multiple senses were engaged. By creating large projection pieces with sound and movement, the viwer must move their head and body to fully sense the experience.

For his BFA graduation project, Peter built an 8ft tall ink drawing on graphine paper. This is a translucent material that was sandwiched between a large wooden frame. When projected light hits the clear paper, it adds an extra depth to the material through a backshadow. The construction of this project took over 3 months of work to complete.
