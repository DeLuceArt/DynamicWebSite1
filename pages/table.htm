Digtial Art

For the majority of 2018, Peter strictly created digital paintings. This was to help him build an illustration portfolio in order to find employment. Do to his dedication to painting on Photoshop 8 hours everday for 6 months, he was able to achieve viral status for one of his illustrations.

The "Red Car for the Red Planet" digital painting went viral in 2018 after being picked up by an Instagram account with half a million followers and getting "liked" by billionaire Elon Musk. His next major success would come a year later in 2019 following the important scientific event of a black hole being photographed for the first time. "A Sky Full of Ghosts" was recieved over 900,000 likes on Instagram and was shared over 50,000 times.

Traditional Art

Prior to 2018, almost all of Peter's work was oil or acrylic paintings. He is profficient in oil, acrylic, charcoal, color pastel, ceramics, woodwork, and graphite. Most of his art is rendered in a realistic way with an emphasis on color and blending. The theme of his work is typically related to his passion for outer space but has also delved into his athletic passion, swimming.

His paintings have been hung in galleries and art festivals across the United States. His Cosmonaut series of oil paintings spent time in the Harrisburg Area Art Association Gallery in Pennsylvania and the Wilmington Art Gallery in Delware. Peter has also had work shown in the Clio Gallery in Washington D.C.
